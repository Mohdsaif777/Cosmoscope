[May 11, 2025] – Signs of Life on Exoplanet K2-18
On May 11, 2025, NASA’s James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) revealed possible signs of life on the exoplanet K2-18b. Scientists detected dimethyl sulfide (DMS) — which, on Earth, is only produced by living organisms — along with methane and carbon dioxide in the planet’s atmosphere. While exciting, researchers advise caution and further study.
[May 9, 2025] – Meteorite in India Linked to Asteroid Itokawa
A study published on May 9, 2025, confirmed that a meteorite that landed in Kopargaon, Maharashtra, shares a similar mineral structure with asteroid Itokawa — sampled by Japan’s Hayabusa mission in 2010. This link offers fresh clues about early solar system material..​
[May 12, 2025] – ‘Flower Moon’ and Micromoon Event
On the night of May 12, skywatchers observed the Flower Moon, the full moon of May. Interestingly, it was also a Micromoon, occurring when the Moon is farthest from Earth, making it appear slightly smaller and dimmer.
[May 6, 2025] – Eta Aquarid Meteor Shower
The Eta Aquarids meteor shower peaked on May 6, producing up to 24 meteors per hour. These shooting stars originate from Halley’s Comet and offered a beautiful spectacle in the pre-dawn skies, especially in the Southern Hemisphere.
[May 5, 2025] – ISRO to Launch EOS-09 Satellite
ISRO plans to launch EOS-09 (RISAT-1B) on May 18, 2025, via the PSLV-C61 mission. This radar imaging satellite is expected to enhance border monitoring and national defense capabilities
